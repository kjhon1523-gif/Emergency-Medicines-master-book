1 Pregnancy Pharmacology

FDA Pregnancy Categories (Old System - Still Relevant):
‚Ä¢ Category A: Controlled studies show no risk (rare: levothyroxine)
‚Ä¢ Category B: Animal studies show no risk, no human studies OR animal studies show risk but human studies show no risk (most antibiotics)
‚Ä¢ Category C: Animal studies show risk, no human studies OR no studies in animals or humans (many emergency drugs)
‚Ä¢ Category D: Evidence of human fetal risk, but benefits may outweigh risks (phenytoin, lithium)
‚Ä¢ Category X: Contraindicated in pregnancy (warfarin, isotretinoin, methotrexate)

2.1 Developmental Pharmacology Principles

üë∂ PEDIATRIC DOSING RULES - NEVER GUESS, ALWAYS CALCULATE:

1. WEIGHT-BASED DOSING (MOST ACCURATE):
‚Ä¢ Dose (mg) = Weight (kg) √ó Dose (mg/kg)
‚Ä¢ Example: Amoxicillin 50 mg/kg/day = 15 kg child √ó 50 mg/kg = 750 mg/day
‚Ä¢ Use ACTUAL weight for emergency drugs, IDEAL body weight for some drugs

2. BODY SURFACE AREA (BSA) DOSING (FOR CHEMOTHERAPY):
‚Ä¢ BSA (m¬≤) = ‚àö[Height (cm) √ó Weight (kg) / 3600]
‚Ä¢ Dose (mg) = BSA (m¬≤) √ó Dose (mg/m¬≤)
‚Ä¢ Most accurate but complex - use calculator

3. AGE-BASED DOSING (WHEN WEIGHT UNKNOWN):
‚Ä¢ Use Broselow tape or length-based system
‚Ä¢ Formulas: Weight (kg) = (Age in years √ó 2) + 8 (for 1-10 years)
‚Ä¢ LAST RESORT - measure/weigh if possible

4. MAXIMUM ADULT DOSE RULE:
‚Ä¢ Pediatric dose should not exceed adult dose
‚Ä¢ Example: Acetaminophen max 1g/dose adult ‚Üí child dose capped at 1g even if weight-based >1g

4.2 Narrow Therapeutic Index Drugs

üíä NARROW THERAPEUTIC INDEX (NTI) DRUGS - SMALL MARGIN FOR ERROR:

DEFINITION: Drugs where small changes in dose or blood concentration can lead to serious therapeutic failures or adverse effects.

COMMON NTI DRUGS:
1. Warfarin (INR target 2-3, bleeding risk if >4.5)
2. Digoxin (therapeutic 0.5-0.9, toxic >2.0 ng/mL)
3. Lithium (therapeutic 0.6-1.2, toxic >1.5 mEq/L)
4. Phenytoin (nonlinear kinetics, small changes cause big level changes)
5. Theophylline (therapeutic 5-15, toxic >20 mcg/mL)
6. Cyclosporine/tacrolimus (transplant rejection vs toxicity)
7. Aminoglycosides (nephro/ototoxicity with small overdose)

SAFETY MEASURES FOR NTI DRUGS:
‚Ä¢ Double-check calculations and doses
‚Ä¢ Therapeutic drug monitoring (TDM)
‚Ä¢ Patient education about adherence and symptoms
‚Ä¢ Avoid automatic substitution (brand/generic)
‚Ä¢ Clear communication between prescribers
‚Ä¢ Computerized alerts for interactions

5.2 High-Risk IV Medications - Maximum Infusion Rates

üíâ CRITICAL IV INFUSION RATES - NEVER EXCEED:

7.2 Compatibility & Incompatibility Guidelines

üß™ CRITICAL IV DRUG INCOMPATIBILITIES:

NEVER MIX IN SAME LINE/SYRINGE:
1. Heparin + antibiotics (especially penicillins, gentamicin) - inactivation
2. Phenytoin + dextrose - precipitation (use NS only)
3. Insulin + any other drug - adsorption to plastic, unpredictable dosing
4. Amiodarone + most other drugs - incompatible with many solutions
5. Sodium bicarbonate + calcium - precipitation (calcium carbonate)
6. Propofol + any other drug - lipid emulsion breaks down

Y-SITE INCOMPATIBILITIES (COMMON):
‚Ä¢ Amphotericin B + electrolyte solutions (precipitate)
‚Ä¢ Ceftriaxone + calcium-containing solutions (precipitate, especially in neonates)
‚Ä¢ Furosemide + acidic solutions (precipitate at pH <5.5)
‚Ä¢ Midazolam + albumin (precipitate)
‚Ä¢ Vancomycin + heparin (precipitate)

SAFETY RULES:
1. When in doubt, use separate IV lines
2. Flush line with NS between incompatible drugs (10-20 mL)
3. Check compatibility references (Trissel's, Micromedex)
4. Monitor for precipitation, cloudiness, color change
5. Use in-line filters for certain drugs (amphotericin, TPN)
